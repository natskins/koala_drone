About Me

Hi, I'm Nathan Smith — an ecologist, science communicator, and licensed RPA (Remotely Piloted Aircraft) operator based in New South Wales, Australia. I hold a BSc in Ecology from James Cook University and a Master’s in Science Communication from Otago University. I am REOC-certified with CASA, ensuring full regulatory compliance for commercial drone operations. With over 15 years of experience in ecology, wildlife filming, and drone piloting, I specialise in detecting threatened arboreal species like koalas and gliders.

What I Do

  • Thermal surveys for koalas, gliders, and other arboreal mammals
  • Drone-based wildlife monitoring and habitat assessments
  • Support for ecological and environmental impact assessments
  • Wildlife cinematography and field documentation

Technology

I use industry-leading equipment including the DJI Matrice platform paired with a Zenmuse thermal camera to deliver reliable and ethical survey results in challenging environments, day or night.

Why Thermal Imaging?

Thermal drones offer fast, accurate, and non-invasive detection of animals in complex or nocturnal environments. This is especially effective for identifying cryptic or canopy-dwelling species that are difficult to detect using traditional survey methods.
